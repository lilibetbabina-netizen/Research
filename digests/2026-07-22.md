# Physics & Maths Digest — 2026-07-22

Collective quantum timekeeping in a semiconductor and a foundations paper on the quantum arrow of time in continuous-spectrum systems.

## Top discoveries

### 1. Non-local synchronization of continuous time crystals
Nature Communications (22 Jul 2026): Greilich et al. show mutual synchronization of continuous time-crystal auto-oscillators in InGaAs electron-nuclear spin systems across spatial separation, locking even when natural frequencies differ by up to ~40%, mediated by spin diffusion.

**Why it matters:** Opens pathways to spin networks and spatially extended macroscopic quantum synchronization in solid-state platforms.

### 2. Arrow of time from continuous spectra in QM
A 22 Jul dated foundations manuscript argues that quantum systems with continuous spectra naturally yield time-asymmetry, equilibrium, loss of coherence, and measurement outcomes consistent with Born rule / von Neumann projection without ad hoc postulates.

**Why it matters:** If robust, it would tidy deep inconsistencies between unitary QM and thermodynamics; still early/low confidence pending broader scrutiny.
