# Physics & Maths Digest — 2026-07-20

Two Nature-family experimental papers: attosecond X-ray absorption of impulsive molecular ionization, and adaptive boson sampling that probes nonlinear photonic dynamics beyond linear optics.

## Top discoveries

### 1. Attosecond response of molecules to impulsive ionization
Nature Physics (20 Jul 2026): Driver et al. use attosecond X-ray absorption spectroscopy on para-aminophenol to resolve ultrafast electron dynamics after sudden ionization, comparing to state-of-the-art theory across subfemtosecond decay and few-femtosecond oscillations.

**Why it matters:** Sudden ionization drives chemistry in atmospheres and space; this measurement benchmarks models of electron correlation and nonadiabatic dynamics beyond Born–Oppenheimer.

### 2. Adaptive boson sampling beyond quantum linear optics
Nature Photonics (20 Jul 2026): Rodari et al. implement adaptive boson sampling with measurement-based feedback on a photonic platform, quantifying a gap versus linear-optical evolution and probing regimes where nonlinear dynamics can arise.

**Why it matters:** Optical nonlinearities are a bottleneck for photonic quantum computing; limited adaptivity becomes a practical testbed for new dynamical regimes.
