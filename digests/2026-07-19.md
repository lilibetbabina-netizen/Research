# Physics & Maths Digest — 2026-07-19

A quieter Sunday bridging the mid-July cluster: mathematical regularity for Navier–Stokes with transport noise, and cosmology links between wineglass wormholes and no-boundary instantons.

## Top discoveries

### 1. Transport noise prevents blow-up in 3D Navier–Stokes (high probability)
An arXiv preprint (16 Jul 2026; digested this quiet Sunday) proves that 3D Navier–Stokes equations driven by a suitably chosen transport noise admit global-in-time smooth solutions with high probability, uniformly for large initial data in subcritical spaces.

**Why it matters:** A major open problem in analysis/PDE is global smoothness of 3D NSE; this shows a physically motivated stochastic perturbation can regularize the dynamics with high probability.

### 2. Wineglass wormholes and no-boundary instantons as one family
Work accepted in Phys. Rev. D (20 Jul) and discussed over the weekend argues Euclidean wineglass wormholes and no-boundary instantons form a common family; small-charge wormholes pinch off into disconnected no-boundary geometries.

**Why it matters:** Clarifies how inflationary universes might nucleate and connects two previously separate semiclassical gravity proposals.
