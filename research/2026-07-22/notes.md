## Findings

### 1. Non-local synchronization of continuous time crystals
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** Nature Communications (22 Jul 2026): Greilich et al. show mutual synchronization of continuous time-crystal auto-oscillators in InGaAs electron-nuclear spin systems across spatial separation, locking even when natural frequencies differ by up to ~40%, mediated by spin diffusion.
- **Why it matters:** Opens pathways to spin networks and spatially extended macroscopic quantum synchronization in solid-state platforms.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-026-75714-1

### 2. Arrow of time from continuous spectra in QM
- **Field:** maths
- **What changed:** A 22 Jul dated foundations manuscript argues that quantum systems with continuous spectra naturally yield time-asymmetry, equilibrium, loss of coherence, and measurement outcomes consistent with Born rule / von Neumann projection without ad hoc postulates.
- **Why it matters:** If robust, it would tidy deep inconsistencies between unitary QM and thermodynamics; still early/low confidence pending broader scrutiny.
- **Confidence:** low
- **Key source:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.19142
