## Findings

### 1. Transport noise prevents blow-up in 3D Navier–Stokes (high probability)
- **Field:** maths
- **What changed:** An arXiv preprint (16 Jul 2026; digested this quiet Sunday) proves that 3D Navier–Stokes equations driven by a suitably chosen transport noise admit global-in-time smooth solutions with high probability, uniformly for large initial data in subcritical spaces.
- **Why it matters:** A major open problem in analysis/PDE is global smoothness of 3D NSE; this shows a physically motivated stochastic perturbation can regularize the dynamics with high probability.
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Key source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15140

### 2. Wineglass wormholes and no-boundary instantons as one family
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** Work accepted in Phys. Rev. D (20 Jul) and discussed over the weekend argues Euclidean wineglass wormholes and no-boundary instantons form a common family; small-charge wormholes pinch off into disconnected no-boundary geometries.
- **Why it matters:** Clarifies how inflationary universes might nucleate and connects two previously separate semiclassical gravity proposals.
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Key source:** https://journals.aps.org/prd/accepted/10.1103/jpwp-2nzv
