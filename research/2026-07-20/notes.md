## Findings

### 1. Attosecond response of molecules to impulsive ionization
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** Nature Physics (20 Jul 2026): Driver et al. use attosecond X-ray absorption spectroscopy on para-aminophenol to resolve ultrafast electron dynamics after sudden ionization, comparing to state-of-the-art theory across subfemtosecond decay and few-femtosecond oscillations.
- **Why it matters:** Sudden ionization drives chemistry in atmospheres and space; this measurement benchmarks models of electron correlation and nonadiabatic dynamics beyond Born–Oppenheimer.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-026-03360-x

### 2. Adaptive boson sampling beyond quantum linear optics
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** Nature Photonics (20 Jul 2026): Rodari et al. implement adaptive boson sampling with measurement-based feedback on a photonic platform, quantifying a gap versus linear-optical evolution and probing regimes where nonlinear dynamics can arise.
- **Why it matters:** Optical nonlinearities are a bottleneck for photonic quantum computing; limited adaptivity becomes a practical testbed for new dynamical regimes.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41566-026-01959-3
