## Findings

### 1. Universal topological gates via anyon braiding and fusion on hardware
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** A Quantinuum / UChicago / Harvard / Stony Brook team prepared a 54-qubit \(S_3\) topological order on Quantinuum’s H2 processor and showed that combining anyon braiding with fusion yields a universal topological gate set, including topological preparation of a magic state. Nature published the result on 15 July 2026.
- **Why it matters:** Universal, potentially more noise-resilient operations are a core missing piece for scalable topological quantum computing; demonstrating them on real hardware moves the approach from theory toward engineering pathways.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10709-y

### 2. Weyl-semimetal nanowires that beat copper when thinner
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** Cornell researchers report single-crystal niobium arsenide (NbAs) nanowires with surface-dominant transport that become better conductors as they shrink — opposite to copper interconnect behavior — remaining robust at room temperature. Reported in Science (16 July 2026); covered 17 July.
- **Why it matters:** Chip interconnects are a hard limit on further transistor scaling; a materials proof that topological semimetals can outperform copper at nanoscale is a concrete capability advance (even if arsenide toxicity may block direct replacement).
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://phys.org/news/2026-07-thinner-wires-faster-electrons-quantum.html

### 3. Room-temperature quantum statistical plasmonic metacrystals
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** LSU-led work in Nature introduces plasmonic metacrystals whose near-field multiparticle dynamics create allowed/forbidden bands that selectively transmit light according to quantum-statistical / coherence properties — at room temperature.
- **Why it matters:** Most quantum materials need cryogenic conditions; a room-temperature platform that sorts and transports quantum states of light could matter for sensing, communications, and eventually energy applications.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10782-3

### 4. Diffusion shown compatible with special relativity (kinetic theory)
- **Field:** maths (mathematical physics)
- **What changed:** A Physical Review Letters paper (8 July 2026) argues that smooth, localized solutions of the diffusion equation arise as particle densities of exact solutions of the relativistic Vlasov–Fokker–Planck equation, so apparent causality/instability problems are artifacts of non-physical solution spaces rather than a fundamental clash with relativity.
- **Why it matters:** Clarifies a long-standing foundations issue used across physics and applied maths modeling; strengthens the theoretical basis for relativistic transport and hydrodynamics.
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Key source:** https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/rkz9-xps2

### 5. Galactic proton PeVatron identified with multi-observatory data
- **Field:** physics
- **What changed:** A Hiroshima-led team combines Fermi-LAT, Chandra, FUGIN, and LHAASO data to identify LHAASO J1912+1014u as a hadronic (proton) PeVatron — an accelerator of extremely high-energy cosmic-ray protons in the Milky Way. Astrophysical Journal, 16 July 2026; covered 17 July.
- **Why it matters:** Pinning down where the Galaxy’s highest-energy protons are made sharpens models of cosmic rays and high-energy astrophysical environments.
- **Confidence:** high
- **Key source:** https://phys.org/news/2026-07-source-extremely-high-energy-particles.html

### 6. Claimed AI proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture (unverified)
- **Field:** maths
- **What changed:** Around 10 July 2026, OpenAI released a preprint claiming GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra produced a proof of the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture (open since the 1970s). Some mathematicians have called the argument promising/elementary; formal peer review and community verification are still ongoing.
- **Why it matters:** If verified, this would be a major graph-theory milestone and a landmark for AI-assisted proof. Until checked, it remains a claim.
- **Confidence:** low
- **Key source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cycle_double_cover_conjecture
